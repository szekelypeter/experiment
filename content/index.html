What is CARP?

What is CARP?

CARP 3D is a cheap, and rapid 3D printer, which operates out of the box, is maintenance-free, and available for everyone. Our goal was to create an affordable, yet fast 3D printer, with high printing quality, and big build volume.

How did we achieve this?

It is simple: we aimed for balance. Most 3D printers are either an overkill, with oversized timing belts, large motors, heavy extruder assembly, bulky and expensive linear technology, and overly complex solutions, OR go for the cheapest in everything, so they use the cheapest steppers, cheap electronics, no linear guides, etc. In our opinion none of the above are a good solution. The first option increases the price of the 3D printer, while the second compromises the printers build quality, or printing speed.

We found a way to incorporate the best of everything. For example: our 3D printer isn't using expensive linear rails, nor wide timing belts, instead we reduced the load on them, allowing us to achieve high speed for a low price. The CARP 3D printer uses big, and strong steppers where it is necessary, and cost effective ones where they don't compromise the performance. We use custom 3D printer electronics, so it is more reliable than an arduino based solution -like ramps-, while it is also cheaper. Our mechanical structure is also simple. We are using as few parts as possible, most of them designed specifically for CARP (there are less than 30 different parts including screws, washers, and nuts). This means that the CARP 3D printer is easy to manufacture, helping us to reduce labour costs. All of this made it possible for us to create one of the cheapest, yet reliable and fast 3D printer.

Specifications

3D printing technology: FDM/FFF

Dimensions: 220*220*350mm

Build volume: 3Litre+ (around 150*140*150mm)

Maximal printing speed: ~150mm/s

Software: custom (also works with standard g-code)

Weight: ~6.5lbs/3kg (so expect low shipping costs)

Filament type: standard 1.75mm filament, up to 185mm spool diameter

Power consumption: <60W, "power brick"

Retail price:(TBA, $250 maximum, making it one of the cheapest printers on the market)

Additional features: automatic calibration, out-of the box operation

Q&A

What is CARP?

CARP is a Cheap And Rapid 3D Printer.

There are plenty of fish in the sea. Why is CARP different than other 3D printers?

CARP is really cheap, yet fast. It has a closed build chamber, custom interface, and a lot of features to provide a good printing experience. CARP 3D comes completely assembled, and ready to print.

You write cheap and fast, but what does that mean?

Cheap means around $250, making CARP one of the cheapest 3D printers. The final price is not yet set, so it might get lower. The printing speed is around 150mm/s, but that can change too (in theory CARP can print up to 400mm/s, but that speed produces inaccurate prints, shaking, and it's really loud, so we limited it).

When can I buy CARP?

We can't promise anything. Subscribe to our mailing list, and we will make sure to get you the date before we launch (We don't spam, don't worry).

Are you sure about this price? Isn't it a "bit" low?

Yes we are. We contacted the manufacturers for the custom parts, and suppliers for the off the shelf ones (such as stepper motors). We are confident we can manufacture the printer for this price, pay the bills, and still have some money left for food, and gas. Also the production is scalable, the low part count of the printer means we don't need a huge warehouse (unless we sell tens of thousands of it). CARP 3D was designed with manufacturability in mind.
